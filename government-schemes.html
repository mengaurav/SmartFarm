PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)

Financial Support Central Scheme

PM-KISAN is a central sector scheme to provide income support to all landholding farmers' families in the country to supplement their financial needs for procuring various inputs related to agriculture and allied activities as well as domestic needs.

Benefits

₹6,000 per year in three equal installments of ₹2,000 each, every four months directly into the bank accounts of eligible farmer families.

Eligibility

  • All landholding farmers' families with cultivable landholding in their names.
  • Small and Marginal Farmers (SMF) with combined landholding up to 2 hectares.
  • Farmers with larger landholdings also eligible as per revised guidelines.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Land Records
  • Bank Account Details
  • KYC Documents

PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana)

Insurance Central Scheme

PMFBY is a crop insurance scheme that aims to reduce the premium burden on farmers and ensure early settlement of crop assurance claims for the full insured sum.

Coverage

Comprehensive risk insurance to cover yield losses due to non-preventable risks such as natural fire and lightning, storm, hailstorm, cyclone, typhoon, tempest, hurricane, tornado, flood, inundation, drought, dry spells, pests and diseases.

Premium Rates

  • Kharif crops: 2% of sum insured
  • Rabi crops: 1.5% of sum insured
  • Annual commercial/horticultural crops: 5% of sum insured

The balance premium will be paid by the government as subsidy.

Eligibility

  • All farmers including sharecroppers and tenant farmers growing notified crops in notified areas.
  • Voluntary for non-loanee farmers.
  • Compulsory for loanee farmers availing Seasonal Agricultural Operations (SAO) loans from financial institutions.

PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana)

Irrigation Central Scheme

PMKSY is a national mission to improve farm productivity and ensure better utilization of water resources. The scheme focuses on creating sources for assured irrigation and adopting precision irrigation technologies.

Components

  • Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP): Focuses on faster completion of ongoing major and medium irrigation projects.
  • Har Khet Ko Pani: Creation of new water sources through minor irrigation, repair, restoration, and renovation of traditional water bodies.
  • Per Drop More Crop: Promotion of micro-irrigation (drip and sprinkler irrigation).
  • Watershed Development: Effective management of runoff water and improved soil & moisture conservation activities.

Subsidy

Up to 55% subsidy for small and marginal farmers and 45% for other farmers for installing micro-irrigation systems like drip and sprinkler irrigation.

Eligibility

All farmers are eligible to apply for the scheme benefits. Priority is given to small and marginal farmers.

PKVY (Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana)

Organic Farming Central Scheme

PKVY is a scheme to promote organic farming in India. It encourages farmers to adopt eco-friendly concept of cultivation reducing dependency on fertilizers and agricultural chemicals to improve soil health.

Benefits

  • Financial assistance for setting up organic farming units.
  • Support for obtaining organic certification.
  • Training on organic farming practices.
  • Marketing assistance for organic produce.

Financial Support

₹50,000 per hectare for a three-year period for adopting organic farming. This includes ₹31,000 as incentive to farmers, ₹11,500 for inputs, and ₹7,500 for infrastructure development.

Implementation

The scheme is implemented in a cluster approach, with each cluster comprising 50 acres. Farmers in a cluster work together and implement organic farming collectively.

e-NAM (Electronic National Agriculture Market)

Marketing Central Scheme

e-NAM is a pan-India electronic trading portal that networks existing APMC mandis to create a unified national market for agricultural commodities. It helps farmers sell their produce online to get better prices through transparent auction process.

Features

  • Online trading platform for agricultural commodities.
  • Transparent price discovery through competitive bidding.
  • Real-time price information to farmers.
  • Online payment facility.
  • Reduced market fees and intermediaries.

How to Access

Farmers can register on the e-NAM portal or mobile app with the help of local mandi officials. They can bring their produce to the nearest e-NAM integrated APMC mandi for quality assaying and online trading.

Coverage

Currently, over 1,000 mandis across India are integrated with the e-NAM platform, with plans to add more mandis in the future.

RKVY (Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana)

Financial Support Central Scheme

RKVY is a state plan scheme that aims to incentivize states to increase public investment in agriculture and allied sectors. It provides flexibility and autonomy to states in planning and executing agriculture and allied sector schemes.

Focus Areas

  • Crop development.
  • Horticulture development.
  • Agricultural mechanization.
  • Natural resource management.
  • Marketing and post-harvest management.
  • Animal husbandry, dairy, and fisheries.
  • Extension and research.

Implementation

States can use RKVY funds to implement projects in the focus areas based on their agricultural needs and priorities. The scheme follows a project-based approach with 60:40 funding pattern between Center and States (90:10 for North-Eastern and Himalayan States).

Eligibility

All farmers can benefit from the scheme through various projects implemented by the state governments. The specific eligibility criteria may vary depending on the project.

How to Apply for Government Schemes

1

Check Eligibility

Review the eligibility criteria for the scheme you're interested in. Make sure you meet all the requirements before proceeding.

2

Gather Documents

Collect all necessary documents such as Aadhaar card, land records, bank account details, and other scheme-specific requirements.

3

Visit Local Office

Visit your nearest Agriculture Department office, Krishi Vigyan Kendra, or Common Service Center for assistance with the application process.

4

Submit Application

Fill out the application form and submit it along with the required documents. You can apply online through the scheme's official website or offline at designated centers.

5

Track Application

Keep track of your application status using the reference number provided during submission. Follow up with the concerned authorities if needed.

Note: The application process may vary slightly for different schemes. Always refer to the official guidelines or consult with local agricultural officers for scheme-specific application procedures.
